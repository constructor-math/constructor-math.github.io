The Mathematics of Cryptography

From simple ciphers to secure internet communication, it's all number theory.

Why does your online banking password feel like gibberish? Because it is backed by math.

Caesar Cipher: The historical origin of encryption using simple alphabetic shifts to secure military communication.

Cryptography is the art of keeping secrets, and at its core it is built on some of the deepest ideas in mathematics. From the humble Caesar cipher to the public-key systems that protect your messages, number theory, modular arithmetic and algebraic structures do the heavy lifting.

Key insight: A one-way function is easy to compute but hard to invert — the foundation of secure encryption.

Wait, So What's Happening?

Every cryptographic algorithm hides in plain sight a mathematical puzzle.

RSA: Understanding how RSA relies on the mathematical difficulty of factoring large semi-prime numbers (the product of two large primes, $P$ and $Q$).

Modular Arithmetic and Congruence: The role of Euler's totient function $\phi(n)$, Greatest Common Divisor (GCD), and modular remainders in creating public and private keys.

Public Key: used by the sender to encrypt the message.
Private Key: kept secret by the receiver to decrypt the message.

Post-Quantum Solutions: Lattice-Based Cryptography

The Geometry of Numbers: Introduction to the "Lattice"—a linear combination of basis vectors in a D-dimensional space.

Hard Mathematical Problems:
• Shortest Vector Problem (SVP): Finding the vector in a lattice closest to the origin.
• Closest Vector Problem (CVP): Finding the lattice point closest to a random point in space.

Learning With Errors (LWE): A method of encryption that adds "noise" or errors to linear equations, making them computationally "hard" to solve even for quantum computers.

Public Key Systems in LWE: How Alice and Bob can communicate using matrices and error distributions to ensure indistinguishable ciphertexts.

Future Outlook

"Harvest Now, Decrypt Later": The risk of adversaries collecting encrypted data today to decrypt it once quantum hardware matures.

PQC Transition: Global mandates are pushing for an immediate shift to Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) to secure long-term data privacy.
