The Constructor University Datathon 2026 invites participants to tackle high-level challenges in causal reasoning and probabilistic modeling. Overview

About Insytes GmbH

Insytes GmbH is a Bremen-based startup founded by Constructor University alumni, working at the intersection of artificial intelligence and industrial data. The company is backed by the EXIST programme of the German Federal Ministry for Economic Affairs, supported through the University of Bremen and BRIDGE.


The team is led by co-founders Karim Abualrish and Varun Patel. Karim is also an active member of the Bremen startup community, helping foster connections between entrepreneurs, researchers, and industry.

Theme & Topic


This year's Datathon is centred on one of the most fundamental questions in data science: not just what is happening in the data, but why. The theme — Causation, Correlation & Causal Inference — challenges participants to move beyond surface-level patterns and build a rigorous understanding of cause and effect.


At the heart of the challenge is causal inference — a framework for reasoning about the effect of interventions and counterfactuals in complex systems. Participants will work with Directed Acyclic Graphs (DAGs) to map out causal relationships between variables, identify confounders, and determine what can and cannot be inferred from observational data alone.


Probabilistic reasoning ties it all together — giving teams the tools to quantify uncertainty, model dependencies, and draw conclusions that are not just statistically sound, but causally meaningful. Whether you are building models, interpreting results, or designing interventions, thinking probabilistically is key to getting the right answer for the right reasons.

Day 1 — April 17: Opening, Talks & Challenge Launch


Day 1 — April 17: Opening, Talks & Challenge Launch


Day 1 sets the stage for the entire Datathon. After registration and a welcome from the organisers, the industry partner Insytes GmbH introduces the challenge problem — giving teams the real-world context, data, and deliverables they'll be working with over the next three days. The afternoon features a keynote talk on Artificial Intelligence by Omar Elshinawy, followed by an academic session on causal inference and probabilistic methods covering DAGs, do-calculus, counterfactuals, and the common pitfalls of correlation vs. causation.

Day 2 — April 18: Team Work & Support


Day 2 — April 18: Team Work & Support


Day 2 is dedicated entirely to the teams. With the challenge framed and the dataset in hand, participants spend the day building, testing, and refining their causal models — supported by mentors from both Insytes GmbH and Constructor University faculty. The morning session focuses on formalising causal hypotheses using DAG structures and developing probabilistic models, while the afternoon shifts toward optimisation, sensitivity analysis, and preparing visualisations. Teams also begin drafting their presentation slides and final report.


The day takes place in the IRC Seminar Room — a smaller, more collaborative setting ideal for focused work and one-on-one mentoring. Lunch is self-arranged on this day.


Day 2 at a glance:

09:00 Team work: model development
12:00 Lunch break (self-arranged)
13:00 Team work: final development & visualisations
17:00 End of Day 2

Day 3 — April 19: Finalisation, Presentations & Awards


Day 3 — April 19: Finalisation, Presentations & Awards


The final day brings everything together. Teams spend the morning putting the finishing touches on their code, report, and slides before the submission deadline at 11:30. All submissions include a code repository, final report, and presentation slides. After lunch, teams take to the stage to present their work to a panel of judges from Constructor University and Insytes GmbH. Each team has 6 minutes to walk through their approach, causal methodology, and results — followexd by a Q&A. Judges evaluate teams on the creativity of their causal approach, technical rigour, real-world applicability, and clarity of presentation.


The event closes with an awards ceremony recognising the top teams, followed by a closing dinner and networking session — a chance to reflect, connect, and celebrate three days of hard work.

The day wraps up with a panel discussion on AI and industry–academia collaboration, before teams dive into their first working session — exploring the dataset, sketching initial causal structures, and mapping out their approach.
